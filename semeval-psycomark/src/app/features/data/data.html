The PsyCoMark Dataset

The PsyCoMark dataset is a novel resource for researchers and NLP practitioners focused on understanding and detecting conspiracy-related content in online conversations. It's built from Reddit submission statements and is ideal for identifying psycholinguistic markers of conspiracy beliefs.

Dataset Statistics

This dataset contains over 4,800 annotated entries, comprising over 4,100 unique Reddit submission statements from more than 190 subreddits.

Annotation Coverage

  • Unique Comments: There are over 4,100 unique Reddit submission statements.
  • Annotation Depth: The dataset includes approximately 4,800 annotations in total. Around 3,500 comments have a single annotation, 550 have two, and 50 have more.
  • Marker Density: Approximately 4,000 comments have at least one psycholinguistic marker annotation.
    • 1,300 comments contain all five marker types.
    • 1,200 comments have four marker types.
    • 900 comments have three marker types.
    • 400 comments have two marker types.
    • 180 comments have one marker type.

Label Distribution

  • Conspiracy Detection: The distribution of the binary conspiracy labels is well-balanced for the training data, with a near-equal number of "Yes" and "No" labels.
    • Yes (Conspiracy): 1,715 instances.
    • No (Not Conspiracy): 2,263 instances.
    • Can't Tell: 877 instances.
  • Marker Extraction: The dataset includes five types of psycholinguistic markers:
    • Actor: 3,639 instances.
    • Action: 3,601 instances.
    • Effect: 2,916 instances.
    • Evidence: 2,854 instances.
    • Victim: 2,378 instances.
  • Inter-annotator agreement: The Krippendorff’s α for the binary conspiracy labels is 0.58, indicating a moderate level of agreement.

Data Composition and Collection

The PsyCoMark dataset is composed of Reddit submission statements—user-written summaries of media submissions. The data was collected between January and March 2025, but the submission statements themselves span from March 2013 to December 2023.

Sampling Strategy

The dataset is a sample of Reddit submission statements, not a comprehensive collection. To ensure a sufficient number of conspiracy-related examples, a specific sampling strategy was employed.

  • The dataset oversamples comments that are likely to contain conspiracy theories.
  • Approximately 1,150 comments (1/4 of the total) were sourced from the r/conspiracy subreddit.
  • The remaining comments were taken from other subreddits with at least ten submission statements.
  • This oversampling means the dataset is not representative of the overall prevalence of conspiracy theories on Reddit.

Preprocessing and Annotation

The raw data underwent several preprocessing steps to prepare it for annotation:

  • Comments were filtered to a length of 160 to 1,000 characters.
  • Markdown formatting was converted to plain text, and URLs were replaced with the token [URL].
  • Any comments containing quotes were removed to focus on the original author's text.
  • The annotation was performed by native English-speaking crowdworkers on the platform Prolific, who were paid an average of over £9 per hour.
  • Each submission statement was annotated with a binary conspiracy label (conspiracy, not conspiracy, or can't tell) and span-level markers for five psychological mechanisms.

Potential Uses

This dataset is designed for a variety of tasks related to computational social science and natural language processing:

  • Training models for conspiracy detection and marker extraction.
  • Studying the linguistic signals of conspiratorial thinking.
  • Cross-topic analysis of conspiracy-related discourse.
  • Explaining and interpreting model decisions by leveraging marker spans.
⚠️ Caution: The dataset oversamples conspiratorial content and should not be used to measure the overall prevalence of conspiracy theories on Reddit or beyond.
